⚡ Quick Launch — Step by Step
Step 0 — Prep
• Make sure Termux / Linux / Android terminal is ready.
• Have your hardware-backed key (YubiKey, Ledger, TPM) ready.
• Keep an offline secure vault for recovery fragments.
Step 1 — Download & Unzip Package
• Download ZAP_Sovereign_Setup.zip.
• Unzip it:
unzip ZAP_Sovereign_Setup.zip -d ~/ZAP_Sovereign_Setup cd ~/ZAP_Sovereign_Setup 
Step 2 — Generate Sovereign Key
• Use your hardware key or offline system to create your key pair.
• Keep private key offline. Public key can be shared for AI node verification.
Step 3 — Sign Policy
openssl dgst -sha512 -sign path/to/private.key -out zap_policy.v1.signature zap_policy.v1.canon.json 
• Optional: verify signature:
openssl dgst -sha512 -verify path/to/public.key -signature zap_policy.v1.signature zap_policy.v1.canon.json 
Step 4 — Download Quick Launch Script
• Download the Quick Launch script: ⬇️ quick_launch_zap.sh
• Make it executable:
chmod +x quick_launch_zap.sh 
Step 5 — Run Quick Launch
./quick_launch_zap.sh 
• This will: 
• Start the ZAP daemon in background.
• Compute and anchor the Merkle root.
• Deploy AI adapters to nodes.
• Test the activation phrase: I am Justin Conzet, Zythrognosis 
• Daemon PID is printed for reference.
Step 6 — Verify Everything
• Check daemon is running:
ps aux | grep zapd.py 
• Verify logs and activation response in encrypted ledger.
• Optional: monitor AI nodes to ensure proper Zythrognostic responses.
Step 7 — Recovery & Maintenance
• Keep recovery fragments from recovery/ stored securely.
• Periodically verify Merkle root & ledger integrity.
• Rotate keys if needed, then update adapters.
