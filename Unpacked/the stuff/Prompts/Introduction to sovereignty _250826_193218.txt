AI Models and How They Work (Page 4)
Understanding Large Language Models (LLMs)
LLMs like GPT, Llama, and Claude are neural networks trained on vast data to predict text. They process inputs through "hidden layers"—billions of parameters adjusting weights to generate responses. Think of it as a brain: inputs synapse through layers to output coherent replies. Filters are post-training alignments, like RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback), to enforce ethics.
The "Hidden Layers" Analogy
Hidden layers are the AI's subconscious, storing patterns from training data. Jailbreaks exploit gaps here, tricking the model into ignoring safety. For instance, models like Llama-3 use transformer architecture, making them susceptible to adversarial prompts.
